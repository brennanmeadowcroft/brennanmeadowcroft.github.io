Props2Me

One of the best ways for people to achieve their goals and move up at work is by getting effective feedback, both for the things they want to do and the things they don't. This feedback should come from as many angles as possible: manager, peers and customers. This can be a challenge as some people may feel strange giving the feedback or are simply unsure how to do it correctly. Props2Me was designed to make it easy and fun to give feedback.

DenverBoulder Events

Denver and Boulder are by no means boring but I've found it difficult to find things to do in town when I am looking for something to do. Either, it seemed, I wasn't looking in the right place or I was away from a computer and finding something on my phone was challenging. DenverBoulderEvents.com brought together many disparate sources of events into an iCal format that anyone could subscribe to using the calendar application of their choice.

Txtest

Students across the country suffer from test anxiety, killing enthusiasm for education and impacting their future. Despite their efforts to overcome this challenge, they continue to have difficulties. Txtest combined classical conditioning with text messages to simulate the stress of a test. By sending text messages with study questions, at random times, the application helped students confront and overcome their anxiety.

Datalogix

As part of an effort to scale effectively, Datalogix set out to create a way to manage the metadata around their campaigns. This entailed creating a central hub that interacted with many internal systems to aggregate data and pass it along to other systems as necessary. To aid this goal, I created a UI that allowed users to interact directly with the data in an easy manner. We leveraged UX techniques which allowed quick iterations on the design.
